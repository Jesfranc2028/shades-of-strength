Even the stars admire her skin.

Like the moon, I don’t shine for attention I glow because it’s who I am. The patches on my skin shimmer in the sunlight and whisper to the stars at night. As a mother, I teach my daughter that beauty isn’t in being the same, but in being true. The stars don’t compete, and neither do I. They simply exist in wonder, just like her.
